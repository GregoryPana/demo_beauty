1) Design Goals

Primary goal: get visitors to click “Order on WhatsApp”.
Secondary goals: trust + clarity (brands supported, how ordering works, quick contact).

This site should feel:

Fast

Clear

Legit

Modern

Not overdesigned

2) Information Architecture

3 pages (Foundation constraint):

Home

Toner Brands

Contact

And one consistent top navigation across all pages.

3) Visual Identity
Color System (CMYK-inspired, printer-friendly)

Use a neutral base + CMYK accents. Keep the colour controlled so it stays professional.

Base

Background: off-white / very light grey (so the colours pop)

Text: near-black (high contrast)

Cards: white with light borders

Accents (CMYK)

Cyan: used for primary buttons/links highlights

Magenta: used for secondary accents (badges, small highlights)

Yellow: used sparingly (icons, small emphasis, caution/attention)

Black: used as a strong anchor (headings, nav, footer)

Important rule:
Don’t plaster all four colours everywhere. Use one primary accent (Cyan) and the others as support accents.

Suggested palette (conceptual)

Ink Black (primary text)

Soft Grey (background + borders)

Cyan (primary action)

Magenta (secondary highlight)

Yellow (micro-accent / info chips)

4) Typography

Toner is utilitarian. Typography should be modern and readable.

Headings: strong sans-serif (bold, slightly condensed feel)
Body: clean sans-serif (comfortable line height)

Style cues:

Headlines should look “tech / supply”

Body text should be calm and clear

5) Layout System
Grid

Desktop: 12-column grid

Max width: ~1100–1200px

Large padding (breathing room)

Shape language

Rounded corners (medium)

Soft shadows (very subtle)

Crisp borders (light grey)

This makes it feel modern and “real” without needing complex visuals.

Short subtext

A product visual (toner cartridge / abstract graphic)

E) Value Proposition Cards (3)

This is your “Why Choose Us” section.

3 cards:

WhatsApp Ordering

Cyan / Magenta / Yellow

G) Footer

Clean and structured:

Social icons (optional, only if you want)

7) Page-by-Page Breakdown
Page 1 — Home (Conversion landing)

Order of sections:

Navbar

Hero

How It Works (3 steps)

Brand Grid

Value Prop Cards

CTA strip

Footer

Keep it simple:

“How do I know which toner I need?”

“Do you have genuine toner?”

This boosts trust without adding complexity.

Page 2 — Toner Brands (SEO + credibility)

Layout:

Page header: “Toner Brands We Supply”

Intro paragraph (SEO-friendly)

Brand sections (accordion or cards)

Recommended structure:
Use accordion panels to keep it neat.

Each brand accordion contains:

Short description

“What to send us” list:

Printer model

Old cartridge code (if available)

CTA button: “Order this brand on WhatsApp”

Add a “Not sure?” helper card at the bottom:

“Send us a photo of your cartridge label on WhatsApp.”

That’s extremely realistic.

Page 3 — Contact

This must be “WhatsApp-first”:

Top section:

Simple contact form (Foundation feature)

Name

Phone

Printer model (optional)

Message

Below:

Operating hours

No map (no storefront).

8) Motion & Interaction (Keep It Minimal)

Because Foundation = structured and not overly animated.

What to include (good)

Navbar shrink on scroll

Smooth scroll to sections

Button hover states

Card hover lift (tiny)

Accordion open/close animation

Optional micro-animation (tasteful)

On scroll, fade-up sections as they enter viewport (very subtle)

Brand cards fade in with slight delay

What NOT to include

Heavy parallax

Complex scroll storytelling

Big text animations

Sticky multi-layer effects

You want “professional small business”, not “agency experimental”.

9) Imagery & Graphics

You have two strong choices:

Option A (best for realism)

Use product photos:

Toner cartridge photo

Close-up “toner powder texture” background (subtle)

Printer tray / office desk photo (generic)

Option B (best for brand identity without photos)

Use vector/3D illustration:

A stylized toner cartridge

CMYK dots / print marks

For your demo, Option B is safer (no real product image licensing issues).
